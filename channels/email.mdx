# Email

Email support uses the same conversation model as live chat, Discord, and Slack.
Inbound email creates or updates conversations, and operators reply from Woes.

## What Email Supports

- Verified sending domains.
- Default from name and local part.
- Reply-to and support mailbox settings.
- Inbound receiving through a Woes forwarding address or a verified sending domain.
- Operator replies from the shared inbox.
- Notification email delivery.
- CSAT and NPS survey emails.

## Configure Email

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings → Channels → Email">
    Review current domain state and email defaults.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a domain">
    Add the domain Woes should send from.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify DNS">
    Complete the DNS records shown in Woes and wait for the domain to become verified.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set routing defaults">
    Configure the local part, sender name, reply-to, and support mailbox.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send a test">
    Use a real verified-domain test email before relying on production support flows.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## DNS Records To Expect

Woes shows the exact DNS values for your domain, but the records usually fall
into these groups:

| Area | Record | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Domain verification | TXT DKIM record | Proves the sending domain belongs to your workspace. |
| Sending | MX and TXT SPF records | Lets Woes send support replies for the domain and handle delivery signals. |
| DMARC | TXT record | Defines domain policy for receiving mail providers. |
| Receiving | MX record | Routes inbound support mail to Woes. |
| Tracking | CNAME record | Enables branded link and open tracking when enabled. |

Use a subdomain such as `support.example.com` when the root domain already has
MX records for Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, or another inbox provider.

## Inbound Email

Email support accepts messages after you choose an answering agent and set up an
intake path. You can forward mail to your Woes forwarding address, or verify a
domain so customers can write to your own support address.

After setup, send a real email to the configured support address and confirm it
appears in **Inbox** as an email conversation. Then reply from Woes and confirm
the customer receives the response from the expected sender.

## Inbound Behavior And Diagnostics

Woes routes inbound email by the verified support address. Messages that cannot
be matched to one workspace are skipped instead of guessed.

Retries from mail providers should not create duplicate customer messages. If
an email does not appear in Woes, check:

- Email support is enabled.
- An answering agent is selected.
- The forwarding address or custom support address is correct.
- The sending domain is verified when you expect Woes to reply from your domain.
- The message is not empty, automated-only, or sent only through `Cc`/`Bcc`.

Attachments are stored as metadata for operator context. Woes does not store or
serve raw attachment contents from inbound email in the current flow.

## Survey Emails

Email surveys use signed score links. A customer clicks a score, Woes verifies the token, validates the score range for the survey type, and records the response.

Configure survey type, timing, delay, and follow-up prompts in
[Survey Settings](/settings/surveys). API route details for survey response
capture live in the API Reference tab.

<Warning>
  Email delivery depends on verified domain configuration. Do not paste email
  provider secrets, private headers, or customer-sensitive content into docs,
  macros, or public replies.
</Warning>
