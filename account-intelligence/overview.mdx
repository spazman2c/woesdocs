# Account Intelligence

Account intelligence helps support teams understand who is asking, what account
they belong to, what has happened before, and what work is still open.

## Sources of Account Context

| Source | What it contributes |
| --- | --- |
| Conversations | Recent support history, channels, status, and customer details. |
| Clients | Account-level aggregate view across conversations and issues. |
| Widget presence | The page or product area where a known customer is active. |
| Custom fields | Workspace-specific account and customer attributes. |
| Issues | Open bugs, requests, blockers, and follow-up work. |

## Operator Uses

- Prioritize high-value or blocked accounts.
- Avoid asking customers to repeat context.
- Find active issues before replying.
- See whether a customer is currently active in the product or docs.
- Group support patterns by account, plan, product area, or custom fields.

## Good Data Habits

- Keep customer names, emails, and account names consistent.
- Use custom fields for attributes that matter to routing or reporting.
- Avoid storing secrets or sensitive identifiers in freeform notes.
- Convert durable requests into issues so account history stays actionable.
