# Webhooks Connector

Webhooks are a planned connector for teams that want Woes events delivered to customer-owned systems without a no-code platform in the middle.

## Status

**Planned connector.** Webhooks should remain disabled until Woes has stable event schemas, signing, delivery retries, per-workspace subscriptions, and operator-visible delivery logs.

## What it will do

A Woes webhooks connector should let teams subscribe external systems to support events:

- New conversation or issue created.
- Issue status, priority, tag, assignment, or SLA changes.
- Survey response received.
- Automation rule completed or failed.
- Agent handoff, low-confidence skip, or human takeover events.

## Product fit

Webhooks are for teams with their own backend, data warehouse, incident tooling, or internal workflow system. They should complement the public REST API: REST lets customers pull or mutate workspace records, while webhooks let Woes push event notifications when something changes.

## Implementation requirements

Before webhooks become active, Woes needs:

- Workspace-scoped endpoint subscriptions.
- Signed payloads and secret rotation.
- Stable event schemas with versioning.
- Retry, backoff, dead-letter, and replay behavior.
- Delivery logs that redact secrets and customer-private fields where needed.
- Rate limits and permission checks for creating or editing subscriptions.

## Early recipes

- Send high-priority support issues into an internal escalation service.
- Mirror resolved issues into a data warehouse for reporting.
- Notify a customer-owned workflow when a survey response is received.
